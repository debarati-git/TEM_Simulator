Theory & Inference

Real ↔ Reciprocal space

Real space holds the periodic atomic lattice. Reciprocal space is its Fourier dual — what the electron beam "sees" after scattering. A crystal with plane spacing d produces a reciprocal-lattice point at distance 1/d from the origin: tighter planes → wider spots.

Why spots appear — Bragg

Each spot corresponds to a family of crystal planes {hkl}. Constructive interference happens only when 2d sin θ = nλ. Spots far from the center come from planes with small d; spots near the center come from planes with large d.

The zone axis [uvw]

The beam direction through the crystal. Only reciprocal-lattice points lying in the plane perpendicular to [uvw] appear in the pattern — that's why pattern geometry changes when you change zone. The pattern's symmetry mirrors the crystal's symmetry along [uvw].

The camera equation
d = λL / R

Measure R on the screen (use the Ruler), read λ from voltage and L from the slider, and the plane spacing d follows directly.

What you can infer
  • Crystal structure — from spot symmetry and systematic absences (FCC misses odd–even mixes; BCC misses h+k+l odd).
  • Lattice parameter a — back-calculated from any measured d via the d-spacing formula.
  • Crystal orientation — the zone axis [uvw] that gives this pattern geometry.
  • Phase identification — match measured d-spacings against a known-material database.

DIFFRACTION LAB How to use this lab
01
What this is

A single-crystal SAED (Selected Area Electron Diffraction) simulator. Pick a crystal and a zone axis, then read off Miller indices and d-spacings from the pattern.

02
Real space — left pane

Drag the lattice to rotate the crystal in 3D. The yellow [100] [010] [001] arrows are anchored to the crystal and spin with it.

03
Reciprocal space — right pane

Click any spot to read its (hkl) indices, d-spacing, |g|, Bragg 2θ, and screen radius R. Use the Ruler tool to measure between two spots and infer d directly.

04
The controls

Camera Length L scales the pattern via R = λL / d. Acc. Voltage sets the electron wavelength λ. Reset snaps the view back to the [001] zone axis.

Scope: this lab simulates single-crystal SAED only. Ring (polycrystalline) patterns and CBED are not included.
