About TEM

The Transmission Electron Microscope images a thin specimen by passing a high-energy electron beam through it. Electromagnetic lenses focus the beam, the specimen modulates it, and a detector captures the resulting image — at resolutions far below a nanometre.

Operating one well requires understanding the column, the alignment sequence, and the optics behind both imaging and diffraction modes. This simulator gives you a safe environment to build that understanding before stepping into the lab.
